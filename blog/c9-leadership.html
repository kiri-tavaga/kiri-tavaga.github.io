Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate leadership?

Cognitive component: "I understand you"

Affective component: "I feel for you"

Motivational component: "I want to help you"

I have had great compassionate leaders in my life that modelled the above three components from their hearts and not really their minds as the suggested training methods in the book.

Regardless of who you are "helping/leading" that person is going to know if you are "real". In my experience I choose to lead compassionately from my heart so that every moment shared with each other (because it's reciprocal-not just one way) has meaning and purpose.

Multiplying Goodness

Taking a moment to remember my compassionate leaders and appreciate the values they have sown in me was a worthwhile exercise.

Tonglen Meditation

Didn't really work for me.

SCARF model in Phase 0 and application at Bootcamp

Status - Not comparing myself to others and just going at my own pace in my own way has helped.

Certainty - Don't sweat the small stuff. I find I compartmentalise things I have to deal with and can park things when necessary.

Autonomy - Believing you are in control till you find that you're actually not. The feeling of helplessness in the face of stress.

This is where my support system is invaluable. My God, My husband and children, My friends - basically my important relationships that will reassure me that everything is going to be OK.

Relatedness - Friend or Foe... Coming from a small town even strangers are friends and whakawhanaungatanga is usually the first topic of conversion. The desire to know how we are connected.

Fairness - Explained in the book is more like pride and ego. I am all for justice and treating others how you want to be treated. There is no room for ego when you put others first.

Difficult conversation

I tend to use the sandwich technique (something positive, the issue and it's effects, then something positive) or ... When you, I felt, Can we.

Both of these approaches have helped with all difficult conversations.
